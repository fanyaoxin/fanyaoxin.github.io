Data preprocessiong techniques for classification without discrimination

  • Discrimination-aware classification problem
    • training data including —— sensitive attributes (discrimination) — gender or ethnicity
    • a classfier taht optimize accuracy, but does not have this discrimination in its predictions
    • relevant in many settings —— data are generated by a biased decision process or when the sensitive attribut serves as a procy代理 for unobserved features.
  • preprocessing techniques
    • suppression of the sensitive attribute
    • massaging the dataset
    • reweighing or resampling the data

introduction

  • quality of the learned models, depends critically on the quality of the training data
    • learn a discrimination-free classifier from discriminatory input data

  • Suppression

    we find the attributes that correlate most with the sensitive attributes S—— we remove S and these most correlated attributes.——— baseline in our experiments

  • Massaging the dataset.

    change the labels of some objects in the dataset in order to remove the discrimination from the input data.——— the select the best candidates for relabeling ——— a ranker is used ——— a Naive Bayesian classifier was used for both the ranking and learning

  • reweighing —— the dataset are assigned weights —— carefully

  • sampling

    calculate the sample size for the 4 combinations of S- and Class-values that would make the dataset discrimination-free. —— Uniform Sampling (US), Preferential Sampling (PS)

motivation

  • scenarios for discrimination-aware classification

    • historical discrimination

      the employment historical data —— biased —— can’t predict the future

    • multiple data source

    • sensitive attributes as a proxy

      ​

  • anti-discrimination legislation

Problem statement

  • measure for discrimination

    a\ set\ of\ attributes : A=\left{A_1,A_2,A_3,…,A_n\right} \quad dom(A_i)
    $$

$$
